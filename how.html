The Rules of the Game

How does WikiPath work?

You start on one Wikipedia article and must reach another — using only the links inside each page. No search bar. No shortcuts. Just your knowledge of how ideas connect.

The basic flow
1 🎯
Get your mission

Two Wikipedia articles are chosen — a start and a target. Your job is to navigate from one to the other.

2 🔗
Click your way through

Read the current article and click any blue underlined link to navigate to that Wikipedia page. Each click counts as one move.

3 ★
Reach the target

Land on the target article to win. The fewer clicks you used and the faster you did it, the higher your star rating.

The rules
Rule 01

You may only navigate by clicking links within the current Wikipedia article. No browser back button, no search bar, no typing URLs.

Rule 02

Each click on a valid internal Wikipedia link counts as 1 move. Your total move count is tracked at the top of the screen.

Rule 03

The goal is to reach the target page in as few clicks as possible. A live timer also runs — finishing fast earns a better rating.

Rule 04

Links to special Wikipedia pages (categories, files, talk pages) are disabled — only article links are clickable.

Rule 05

You have 3 hints per game. A hint highlights a link on the current page that points toward the target article.

Difficulty levels
E 🟢
Easy

Everyday topics — Pizza, Tokyo, Music, Gold. Short distances between articles, well-connected pages. Great for first-timers.

M 🟡
Medium

History, science, and culture — Darwin, Jazz, Empire, Relativity. Requires broader knowledge and more deliberate navigation.

H 🔴
Hard

Obscure and specialised — Ribosome, Topology, Tardigrade, Quasar. Fewer obvious paths. Only for the well-read and the brave.

Tips & strategy
Tip 01
Think in categories

Most topics connect through broad hubs — countries, historical periods, scientific fields. Navigate toward those first, then zoom in.

Tip 02
Geography is your friend

Country and city articles are densely linked. If you're stuck, find a geographic connection — it usually opens up new paths quickly.

Tip 03
Save hints for dead ends

You only get 3 hints per game. Don't waste them early — hold them for when you're genuinely stuck on a sparsely-linked article.

Tip 04
Skim, don't read

You're not here to learn (well, not only). Skim headings and the first paragraph for relevant links rather than reading every word.
